GitHubCopilot moves to token-metered "AI Credits" on 1 June — the end of flat-fee AI coding

28 April 2026 · Primary source: GitHub Blog

GitHub announced that every Copilot plan — Pro, Pro+, Business, and Enterprise — will switch from request-based pricing to usage-based "GitHub AI Credits" on 1 June 2026. Credits are deducted by input + output + cached tokens at each model's listed API rate, with included monthly allotments matching list price ($10 of credits on Pro, $39 on Pro+) and bridge allotments for seats ($30/seat on Business and $70/seat on Enterprise from June through August). Subscription prices stay flat. Crucially, code completions and Next Edit suggestions remain free of credits on every plan; agentic and chat-style work is what drains the meter. GitHub frames the change as forced by Copilot's evolution into an agent platform that runs long, multi-step coding sessions; developer reaction across X and Hacker News so far has been "you'll get less for the same price." A preview-bill experience lands in early May so users can pressure-test their projected June bill.

Hugging Facetransformers 5.7.0 ships Mistral 4, Jina v3 embeddings, and a multimodal/OCR sweep

28 April 2026 · Primary source: huggingface/transformers GitHub release

Hugging Face cut transformers v5.7.0 as the first major minor-version release on the v5 PyTorch-first line. The release adds Mistral 4, Jina-Embeddings-v3 (multilingual, 5 task-specific LoRA adapters built in), VidEoMT for online video segmentation, UVDoc for document rectification, PI0 robotics policies, SLANeXt, and PP-OCRv5. Quietly more impactful: speedups across quantization, tokenization, kernels, caching, and tensor/pipeline parallelism, plus several breaking changes around attention masks and generation defaults that consumers of v5.x should review before upgrading. The combination — a multimodal sweep plus a parallelism overhaul — positions transformers as the integration target of choice for vision-language and document-AI workloads heading into Q2.

AppleiOS 26 SDK becomes mandatory for every App Store submission

28 April 2026 · Primary source: Apple Developer

As of 28 April 2026, App Store Connect rejects new submissions that aren't built against the iOS 26 / iPadOS 26 / tvOS 26 / visionOS 26 / watchOS 26 SDK or later — meaning Xcode 26 is the new floor for shipping. The cutover doesn't force an iOS 26 deployment target on the app itself (developers can still target older OS versions for runtime), but it does compel every active iOS team to migrate build pipelines, CI runners, and provisioning profiles this week. Combined with the Liquid Glass design language and Apple Intelligence on-device APIs that landed with iOS 26, today's deadline operationalises Apple's AI-era platform reset across the long tail of App Store apps.

Manifest OS$60 M Series A at $750 M valuation — largest legal-tech Series A on record

28 April 2026 · Primary source: Business Wire / Manifest OS

Manifest OS closed a $60 M Series A at a $750 M valuation, which the company calls the largest Series A in legal-tech history. The deal lands the same week as Avoca's $1 B home-services agent round and reinforces the broader Q2 thesis: vertical agentic AI that books real revenue is now where the late-Series A capital is concentrating.
