IBMIBM Bob: AI development partner ships globally, eyeing the production-readiness gap

IBM made IBM Bob generally available worldwide today, positioning it as a full-stack alternative to single-purpose code copilots. Bob spans the entire SDLC — planning, coding, testing, deployment, modernization — with persona-based agents, multi-model orchestration, reusable playbooks, an auditable shell ("BobShell"), and human-in-the-loop governance baked in. GitHubApp installation tokens move to a new, longer, stateless format

27 April 2026 · Primary source: GitHub Changelog

GitHub began a multi-week staged rollout today that changes the format of newly minted GitHub App installation tokens — from the long-stable 40-character ghs_* form to a new ghs_APPID_JWT structure of roughly 520 characters. The change is meant to improve token-issuance performance and API reliability under load. The risk: any application, action, or integration that hard-codes a 40-character length check will silently break. The rollout starts with GitHub Actions GITHUB_TOKEN and first-party integrations (Dependabot, Slack, Teams) and extends to all GitHub App tokens between mid-May and late-June 2026.

Ineffable IntelligenceDavid Silver's "no-human-data" superlearner emerges with a $1.1 B European seed record

27 April 2026 · Primary source: Ineffable Intelligence / UK Government

AlphaGo / AlphaZero / AlphaStar / AlphaProof co-architect David Silver publicly launched Ineffable Intelligence in London with a $1.1 B seed round at a $5.1 B valuation — the largest European seed on record. Sequoia and Lightspeed co-led, with NVIDIA, DST Global, Index, Google, and the U.K.'s Sovereign AI Fund participating. The thesis is a hard reset on how frontier systems are trained: a superlearner built entirely from reinforcement learning, with no pre-training and no human-generated text, on the bet that today's internet-pretraining paradigm has hit a knowledge ceiling. The founding team is largely ex-DeepMind (Wojciech Czarnecki, Lasse Espeholt, Junhyuk Oh). Silver has pledged 100% of his founder equity to philanthropy via Founders Pledge.

ChinaNDRC formally blocks Meta's $2 B acquisition of agentic-AI startup Manus

27 April 2026 · Primary source: NDRC / Bloomberg / NPR

China's National Development and Reform Commission ordered Meta to unwind its previously announced $2 B (some reports up to $2.5 B) acquisition of Manus, the Singapore-headquartered agentic-AI startup with Beijing roots. Manus had relocated to Singapore in mid-2025; its parent (Butterfly Effect) was Beijing-based, and CEO Xiao Hong and chief scientist Ji Yichao were reportedly barred from leaving China during the regulatory review. The decision is the first time Beijing has formally blocked a cross-border deal explicitly tied to agentic-AI capabilities, and it signals a strategic priority on retaining domestic agent IP at exactly the moment OpenAI, Anthropic, and Google are productizing workspace agents. Meta said the transaction "complied fully with applicable law."
