GitHubInstallation-token format change starts rolling out today — ~520-char tokens, breaks 40-char assumptions

Rollout begins 27 April 2026 · Primary source: GitHub Changelog

GitHub starts a staged rollout today of a new format for App installation tokens. The new shape is ghs_APPID_JWT, with a longer, variable length around ~520 characters, replacing the legacy fixed 40-character format. Phase 1 (27 April – mid-May) covers GITHUB_TOKEN issued by Actions, plus first-party integrations like Dependabot, Slack, and Teams; phase 2 (mid-May – late June) covers all other App installation tokens. Existing tokens keep working until they expire. Any internal tooling, secret-scanning regex, or CI guardrail that hard-codes a 40-character expectation needs to be updated this week — this is precisely the kind of silently-failing migration that surfaces only when a deploy hangs at 2 a.m.

Meta × BroadcomMulti-gigawatt MTIA expansion fleshes out the post-NVIDIA hyperscaler stack

14–26 April 2026 · Primary source: Broadcom Investor Relations / Meta AI

Broadcom and Meta extended their MTIA (Meta Training and Inference Accelerator) partnership to cover multi-gigawatt deployments of Meta's custom silicon, with an initial commitment exceeding 1 GW. MTIA 300 is in production today; MTIA 400 exits qualification soon; MTIA 450 and MTIA 500 are slated for early 2027. Broadcom's Ethernet fabric provides the scale-up, scale-out, and scale-across networking, and the 400/450/500 share chassis, rack, and network infrastructure — meaning each generation drops into existing data centers with no facility re-fit. Read alongside this week's Anthropic-AWS Trainium expansion, the headline trend is that all three U.S. hyperscalers (Google TPU, Amazon Trainium, Meta MTIA) now have credible, in-production silicon that does not depend on NVIDIA H/B-series GPUs.

CaliforniaSB 1000 (AI Transparency Act) hits Senate Appropriations today

Hearing scheduled 27 April 2026 · Primary source: California Legislature

SB 1000, the proposed amendment to California's existing AI Transparency Act, is scheduled in Senate Appropriations today. The bill amends Business and Professions Code §§22757.1–22757.5 to expand provenance-data and disclosure obligations for generative-AI providers operating in California, after clearing the Senate Privacy committee on 13 April. Combined with the package of California AI laws already taking effect this year, SB 1000 would push California's enforcement posture closer to the EU AI Act's transparency tier — and would land on every U.S.-facing model provider that ships to California users, regardless of headquarters. Anyone shipping a consumer model in the U.S. should track this hearing's outcome before the May fiscal-deadline window closes.
