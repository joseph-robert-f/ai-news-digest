OpenAIAgents SDK gets a native sandbox and model-native harness

15–16 April 2026 · Primary source: OpenAI

OpenAI shipped a substantial update to its Agents SDK: a model-native harness and a native sandbox that can run on the same host as the agent or be isolated to a separate machine for stronger durability and security guarantees. The release also adds configurable memory, standardized integrations, portable-workspace support, and built-in snapshotting for long-running agents. Python ships first; TypeScript support, plus code mode and subagents, will follow. The update targets enterprises that have been blocked on rolling out agents by security review.

15–16 April 2026 · Primary source: Gemini CLI docs / Google Developers Blog

Policy, Legal & Risk

LegalCompeting federal rulings carve out the privilege status of AI chats

15–16 April 2026 · Primary source: U.S. District Courts (SDNY, E.D. Mich.)

Two federal decisions landing in the same 24-hour window set up the first real circuit-level tension over AI-chat discovery. In United States v. Heppner, SDNY Judge Jed Rakoff held that a defendant's conversations with Anthropic's Claude were not shielded by attorney-client privilege or the work-product doctrine — reasoning that an AI tool is not an attorney and owes no duty of confidentiality. Hours later, an Eastern District of Michigan magistrate judge ruled that a pro se plaintiff did not have to hand over her ChatGPT chats in an employment suit, treating them as protected personal work product. More than a dozen major U.S. firms rushed client advisories warning that AI chats should now be assumed discoverable unless run through a privileged channel.
