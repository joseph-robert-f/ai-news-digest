BeijingHumanoid robot breaks the human half-marathon world record

19 April 2026 · Primary source: Beijing Municipal Government / China Media Group

At the second Beijing E-Town Humanoid Robot Half-Marathon, an autonomous humanoid running for Honor completed the 21 km course in 50:26 (~25 km/h average), finishing ahead of the top human entrant and beating Jacob Kiplimo's current men's world record of 57:20. A record 112 teams competed, including five international teams from Germany, France and Brazil; autonomous-navigation entries accounted for ~40% of the field. Last year's inaugural winner needed more than 2h 30m on the same course — a staggering year-over-year delta in endurance, balance, and onboard perception.

OpenAIGPT-Rosalind: first Life Sciences model, limited-access research preview

17–18 April 2026 · Primary source: OpenAI

OpenAI introduced GPT-Rosalind, a reasoning model purpose-built for biology, drug discovery, and translational medicine, named for DNA-structure pioneer Rosalind Franklin. The model was trained against 50 of the most common biological workflows with direct access to major public biological databases; OpenAI reports best-of-ten scores above the 95th percentile of human experts on sequence-prediction tasks and ~84th percentile on generation. It launches as a U.S.-only research preview under a Trusted Access program, with Amgen, Moderna, the Allen Institute, and Thermo Fisher Scientific among the first partners. A Life Sciences plug-in for Codex ships alongside it.

AnthropicClaude Design turns Opus 4.7 into a prototype & deck generator

17–18 April 2026 · Primary source: Anthropic

Anthropic launched Claude Design, an experimental product that converts text prompts into interactive prototypes, pitch decks, slides, one-pagers, and UI mockups — targeted at founders and PMs who lack a design background. CloudflareAgents Week closes with managed Agent Memory in private beta

13–18 April 2026 · Primary source: Cloudflare

Cloudflare wrapped up Agents Week 2026 with the private beta of Agent Memory, a managed service that extracts, verifies, classifies, and stores memories from agent conversations so they can be retrieved without bloating the context window. It's accessible via Worker bindings or a REST API, with exportable data by default. The week also delivered network-performance improvements tuned for agentic traffic patterns and a consolidated "agent-first" release bundle spanning Workers AI, Durable Objects, and the Agents SDK — Cloudflare's clearest push yet to become the default control plane for long-running agents.

AI Hardware & Compute

TeslaAI5 tape-out: dual-sourced at Samsung Taylor and TSMC Arizona

15–16 April 2026 · Primary source: Elon Musk / X

Elon Musk confirmed on X that Tesla's in-house AI5 self-driving accelerator has completed tape-out and been sent to fabrication. Production will be split between Samsung's Taylor, Texas fab and TSMC's Arizona fab — an all-U.S. footprint that aligns with the Trump-era CHIPS posture. Tesla claims ~40× total uplift over AI4 (roughly 8× compute and 9× memory), up to 192 GB of LPDDR5X from Samsung, with SK hynix supplying additional memory. Volume production is slated for late-2026 to mid-2027 — nearly two years behind Musk's original schedule.
