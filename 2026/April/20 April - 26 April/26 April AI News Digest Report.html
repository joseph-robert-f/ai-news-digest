DeepSeekV4 Pro & V4 Flash launch: Hybrid Attention, 10% of V3.2's KV cache at 1 M tokens

24 April 2026 · Primary source: DeepSeek API Docs / Hugging Face

DeepSeek released its V4 Pro (1.6 T-parameter MoE) and V4 Flash models open-weights on Hugging Face, claiming top-tier coding, world-knowledge, and agentic-task scores among open-source systems. The headline architectural change is Hybrid Attention, which composes Compressed Sparse Attention (KV-cache compression at 4×, then sparse top-k retrieval via a lightning indexer; top-1024 in V4-Pro, top-512 in V4-Flash) with Heavily Compressed Attention. Net effect at 1 M-token context: ~27% of V3.2's per-token inference FLOPs and ~10% of its KV cache. Coming a year to the day after V3 broke the foundation-model price floor, V4 sets a new bar for cost-efficient long-context open weights.

ICLR 2026Outstanding Paper: “Transformers are Inherently Succinct”

23–26 April 2026 · Primary source: ICLR Blog / OpenReview

ICLR 2026 in Rio de Janeiro (main conference 22–25, workshops 26–27) handed an Outstanding Paper Award to Bergsträßer, Cotterell & Lin for a theoretical result quantifying why transformers work: they can encode formal languages exponentially more succinctly than LTL formulas and RNNs (including state-of-the-art State-Space Models) and doubly exponentially more succinctly than finite automata. As a corollary, verifying even simple properties of a transformer is EXPSPACE-complete — a formal grounding for why mechanistic interpretability is so hard. The committee recognised three Outstanding Papers and one Honorable Mention. Of separate note: an analysis of accepted submissions shows most ICLR 2026 papers were authored in China, while most top-recognized papers came from the U.S. — a divergence worth tracking.
