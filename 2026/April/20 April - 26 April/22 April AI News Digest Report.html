ICLR 2026Ten Outstanding Papers revealed ahead of Rio opening — DPO itself goes on trial

21 April 2026 · Primary source: ICLR / OpenReview

ICLR 2026 released its 10 Outstanding Paper (Oral) selections ahead of the main program, which runs 23–27 April at Riocentro in Rio de Janeiro. The most talked-about entry is “Why DPO is a Misspecified Estimator and How to Fix It” (Gopalan, Ray Chowdhury, Banerjee; arXiv 2510.20413), which argues that Direct Preference Optimization — the default RLHF-replacement across most open-source labs — is statistically misspecified, and proposes a corrected estimator. SafeDPO offers a constrained alternative that balances helpfulness and safety without auxiliary reward networks. Other winners include Common Corpus (ethically-sourced LLM pre-training data), Q-RAG (multi-step retrieval via RL-trained embedders), FIRE (stability–plasticity reinitialization), WebDevJudge (LLM-as-a-judge for web development), and MedAgentGym (agentic biomedical training). The theme across the winners is a decisive shift from representation learning toward trajectory-level evaluation and alignment primitives.

Securitymarimo pre-auth RCE (CVE-2026-39987) under mass exploitation — 10 hours from disclosure to working exploit

8–16 April 2026 · Primary sources: Sysdig Threat Research, Endor Labs, Resecurity

A CVSS 9.3 pre-authenticated RCE in marimo — the open-source reactive Python notebook now embedded in many AI workflows — is under active exploitation. CVE-2026-39987 lives at the /terminal/ws WebSocket endpoint, which only checks running mode and platform support and completely skips validate_auth(), handing any unauthenticated attacker a full PTY shell. Sysdig Threat Research observed real-world exploitation 9h 41m after public disclosure on 8 April, with credential theft completed in under three minutes; 662 exploit events were recorded 11–14 April from 11 unique source IPs across 10 countries. Attackers drop a new variant of NKAbuse, a Go-based malware that abuses the NKN decentralized peer-to-peer protocol for C2, and target .env files and SSH private keys. All versions up to and including 0.20.4 are vulnerable; maintainers shipped the fix in 0.23.0. Given marimo’s AI-dev use profile, the compromise radius overlaps heavily with the supply-chain stories from last week (Vercel / Context.ai, axios npm).
