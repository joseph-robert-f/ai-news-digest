UCLQuantum-informed AI beats classical ML on turbulence with 100× less memory

17–18 April 2026 · Primary source: Science Advances / UCL

A team led by Prof. Peter Coveney at UCL published a paper in Science Advances showing that an AI model trained with features extracted from a 20-qubit IQM quantum computer (linked to classical HPC at LRZ Munich) improves long-term turbulence forecasts by ~20% while requiring roughly 100× less memory than the classical baseline. The authors frame it as the first practical demonstration of quantum advantage on a messy real-world dynamical system — applicable to climate/weather, cardiovascular flow modelling, and wind-farm siting. The result lands in the same week Google and Oratomic reported AI-accelerated quantum algorithms shortening the post-quantum cryptography timeline, suggesting quantum-assisted ML is graduating from demo to meaningful scientific tool faster than the 2024 consensus.
