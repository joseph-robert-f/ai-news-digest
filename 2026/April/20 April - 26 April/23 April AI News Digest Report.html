AI Hardware & Compute

Google8th-gen TPUs split: Sunfish (8t, training) & Zebrafish (8i, inference) on TSMC 2nm

22 April 2026 · Primary source: Google Cloud

For the first time, Google's 8th-generation TPU splits into two specialised silicon lines: TPU 8t (“Sunfish”), a Broadcom-designed training chip that scales to 9,600 TPUs and 2 PB of shared HBM per superpod, claiming 3× the processing power of Ironwood and up to 2× perf/W; and TPU 8i (“Zebrafish”), a MediaTek-designed inference chip that ties 1,152 TPUs per pod with 3× more on-die SRAM for low-latency, massively concurrent agent serving. Both target TSMC 2nm with late-2027 availability. Google also moved the 7th-gen “Ironwood” TPU to GA at Next, closing one of the longest-standing “when-does-it-actually-ship” questions from last year's roadmap.
