AI Hardware & Compute

GoogleTPU v8 splits into “Sunfish” training & “Zebrafish” inference chips

22 April 2026 · Primary source: Google Cloud / Google Blog

At Google Cloud Next 2026 (Las Vegas, 22–24 April), Google made its seventh-generation Ironwood TPU generally available and previewed an eighth generation that is deliberately bifurcated. TPU v8t “Sunfish”, designed with Broadcom on a TSMC 2 nm node, is aimed at training and claims 2.8× better price/performance than Ironwood at up to 121 exaflops per pod. TPU v8i “Zebrafish”, designed with MediaTek, is optimised for agent-scale inference with an advertised 80% better performance-per-dollar. Google also confirmed co-development deals with Marvell, giving it a four-partner silicon supply chain and the clearest strategic answer yet to NVIDIA's inference dominance. SemiAnalysis noted that the split is “the most significant architectural decision in Google's TPU history.”

GitHubCopilot Pro sign-ups paused as agentic coding breaks flat-rate economics

20–22 April 2026 · Primary source: GitHub Blog

GitHub paused new sign-ups for Copilot Pro, Pro+, and Student plans on 20 April and has now published a fuller rationale: long-running agentic workflows routinely consume more compute per user per month than the fixed subscription fee covers, and weekly Copilot cost-of-goods has doubled since the start of 2026. Copilot Free remains open to new users. GitHub also tightened usage caps (Pro+ now gets >5× the limits of Pro), pulled Opus models from the Pro tier (still available on Pro+), and surfaced live usage meters in VS Code and the Copilot CLI. The shift is the clearest public admission yet that the first generation of all-you-can-eat coding-assistant pricing is dead; expect usage-based or tiered token models to follow across competing IDE agents.
