SecurityVercel confirms breach traced to infostealer infection at AI tooling vendor Context.ai

20 April 2026 · Primary source: Vercel Security Bulletin

Vercel updated its April 2026 security bulletin on 20 April confirming a limited customer-data exposure tied to a third-party compromise at AI platform Context.ai. Investigators at Hudson Rock traced the root cause to a Lumma Stealer infection on a Context.ai employee in February 2026 that exfiltrated Google Workspace credentials plus secrets for Supabase, Datadog, and Authkit; attackers used the stolen support@context.ai account to pivot into a Vercel employee’s Google Workspace OAuth session and then into Vercel’s internal environment. Vercel says it coordinated with Microsoft, GitHub, npm, and Socket and found no evidence that Vercel-published npm packages were tampered with. A threat actor using the ShinyHunters persona is reportedly selling the stolen data on BreachForums for $2 million. CEO Guillermo Rauch called the actors “highly sophisticated and likely significantly accelerated by AI.” The incident continues an April pattern (LiteLLM, Axios) of attackers targeting developer-stored credentials across CI/CD, package registries, and OAuth integrations.
