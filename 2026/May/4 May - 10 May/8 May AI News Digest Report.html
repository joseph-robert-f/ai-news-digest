OpenAIGPT-Realtime-2, -Translate, -Whisper: voice models that reason and translate as you speak

7 May 2026 · Primary source: OpenAI

OpenAI announced a new generation of realtime audio models in the API: GPT-Realtime-2 (the first voice model with GPT-5-class reasoning, 128k context), GPT-Realtime-Translate (live speech-to-speech across 70+ input languages and 13 output languages), and GPT-Realtime-Whisper (streaming STT). Together they push voice apps from call-and-response toward agents that listen, reason, and take action mid-conversation — squarely targeting the same "ambient agent" envelope DeepMind, ElevenLabs, and Anthropic have been circling.

ZyphraZAYA1-8B: first reasoning MoE pretrained end-to-end on AMD MI300X

6 May 2026 · Primary source: Zyphra

Zyphra released ZAYA1-8B, an Apache-2.0 mixture-of-experts model with 760M active / 8.4B total parameters, trained end-to-end on a 1,024-GPU AMD MI300X cluster with Pensando Pollara interconnect — the first frontier-class MoE pretrained, midtrained, and SFT'd entirely on AMD silicon. On math and reasoning benchmarks it matches or beats open-weight models more than 14× its active size (Nemotron-3-Nano-30B-A3B, Mistral-Small-4-119B) and stays within reach of first-generation reasoning frontier models like DeepSeek-R1-0528 and Gemini 2.5 Pro. The release introduces Compressed Convolutional Attention (CCA), an MLP-based expert router for routing stability, learned residual scaling, and Markovian RSA — a parallel-trace test-time compute method designed for unbounded reasoning at constant memory. Weights are on Hugging Face; an 80B follow-up is queued.

Cybersecurity & AI Safety

OpenAIGPT-5.5-Cyber: a less-guardrailed model for vetted cyber defenders

7 May 2026 · Primary source: OpenAI

OpenAI opened a limited preview of GPT-5.5-Cyber — a tuned variant of GPT-5.5 with reduced guardrails — to teams cleared into the highest tier of its Trusted Access for Cyber program. Approved defenders can use the model for vulnerability discovery, patch validation, malware reverse-engineering, and red-team simulations that the public model refuses; classes of behavior tied to credential theft and offensive malware authoring remain blocked. The move is OpenAI's most explicit answer yet to Anthropic's Mythos (which earlier disclosed strong network-vulnerability discovery capability) and lands the same week the White House said it's drafting an FDA-style vetting executive order built around exactly that risk profile.

GitHubThree new Enterprise Server CVEs — including the first critical RCE found with AI-assisted reverse engineering

7 May 2026 · Primary source: GitHub Advisories / Wiz Research

GitHub disclosed two new GitHub Enterprise Server vulnerabilities on 7 May: CVE-2026-7541, an unauthenticated DoS via deeply nested JSON payloads on a public API endpoint, and CVE-2026-8106, a reflected HTML injection on the Management Console login page that could enable credential theft. They follow the higher-impact CVE-2026-3854, a critical RCE in GitHub's internal git infrastructure (affecting both GitHub.com and GHES) reported by Wiz Research: an injection flaw in GitHub's internal protocol let any authenticated user execute arbitrary commands on backend servers via a single git push. Wiz's writeup emphasises that the bug was uncovered through AI-augmented reverse engineering (notably IDA MCP) of closed-source binaries — a methodology that researchers and defenders are flagging as the start of a new wave of vulnerability discovery against compiled, proprietary code.

Geopolitics & Regulation

EUAI Act Omnibus deal: high-risk rules slip to Dec 2027, full ban on AI nudification

7 May 2026 · Primary source: European Commission / European Parliament

The European Parliament and Council reached a political agreement on the Digital Omnibus on AI, the most significant rewrite of the EU AI Act since adoption. Two structural changes: (1) simplification of high-risk obligations across biometrics, critical infrastructure, education, employment, migration, asylum and border control — with the application date for those rules postponed by ~16 months to 2 December 2027 (from August 2026); and (2) a hard, EU-wide ban on AI "nudification" apps and the AI-generated creation of CSAM or non-consensual intimate depictions of identifiable persons, with companies required to bring systems into compliance by 2 December 2026. Industry calls it "companies should not be regulated twice"; civil-society groups call the nudification ban "a pivotal moment." A separate Code of Practice is still on track for finalisation by June 2026.

6–7 May 2026 · Primary source: Bloomberg / The Hill

Kevin Hassett, Director of the National Economic Council, said the White House is "studying, possibly an executive order" that would create a pre-release vetting regime for new AI models, framed explicitly as analogous to FDA drug approval. The proximate trigger is Anthropic's disclosure of Mythos's strong network-vulnerability discovery capability and the parallel run-up of OpenAI's GPT-5.5-Cyber tier (above). On the same day, the Commerce Department announced an expansion of its voluntary pre-deployment testing program, with Google, Microsoft, xAI, OpenAI, and Anthropic agreeing to give the U.S. government access to evaluate model capabilities and safety. It is not yet decided whether the contemplated EO would mandate testing — a step that would mark a significant shift away from the Trump administration's previously hands-off AI posture.
