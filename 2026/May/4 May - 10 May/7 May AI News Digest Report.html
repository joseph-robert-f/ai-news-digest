Frontier Models & Safety

OpenAIGPT-5.5 Instant becomes the new default ChatGPT model

5 May 2026 · Primary source: OpenAI

OpenAI began rolling out GPT-5.5 Instant as the new default model in ChatGPT, retiring GPT-5.3 Instant. According to OpenAI's own evaluations, the new model produces 52.5% fewer hallucinated claims than 5.3 Instant on high-stakes prompts in medicine, law, and finance, and 37.3% fewer inaccurate claims on user-flagged challenging conversations. It is also more concise — ~30% fewer words and ~29% fewer lines per response — and gains the ability to reach back into past conversations, files, and Gmail (Plus / Pro web users first) for personalized answers. Developers get it via the API as chat-latest; GPT-5.3 remains available to paying API users for a three-month deprecation window. The release lands roughly two weeks after GPT-5.5 (the reasoning sibling) shipped on 23 April.

GovernmentCAISI signs frontier-AI national-security testing agreements with Google DeepMind, Microsoft & xAI

5 May 2026 · Primary source: NIST / CAISI

The Commerce Department's Center for AI Standards and Innovation (CAISI), housed at NIST, signed memoranda with Google DeepMind, Microsoft, and xAI covering pre-deployment evaluations and targeted national-security research on frontier models — including the ability for evaluations to run inside classified environments. CAISI says it has now completed more than 40 such evaluations to date, including on state-of-the-art models that remain unreleased. The new agreements extend the framework that CAISI's predecessor signed with OpenAI and Anthropic in 2024 to effectively the entire U.S. frontier-model field, and slot into the Trump administration's broader push to put AI oversight under Commerce rather than the EU-style independent regulator.

CopilotKit$27 M Series A for AG-UI — the “agents-inside-apps” protocol

5 May 2026 · Primary source: CopilotKit

Seattle-based CopilotKit announced a $27 M Series A ($20 M new + $7 M previously unannounced seed) led by Glilot Capital, NFX, and SignalFire. The interesting bet is the AG-UI (Agent-User Interaction) protocol — a third pillar alongside Anthropic's MCP (model-to-context) and Google's A2A (agent-to-agent) standards — that lets agents take actions and render UI inside existing apps instead of in a separate chatbox. The company reports millions of installs per week and production deployment at Deutsche Telekom, Docusign, Cisco, and S&P Global, alongside the launch of a self-hostable CopilotKit Enterprise Intelligence bundle. Notable for builders: AG-UI is a credible candidate to become the user-interaction layer of the agent stack, the way MCP has become the data layer.

Google DeepMindUK staff vote 98% to unionize over Pentagon AI deal — first frontier-AI lab union

5–6 May 2026 · Primary source: CWU / Unite the Union

Roughly 1,000 Google DeepMind employees in London voted by an internal poll — 98% in favor — to unionize jointly with the Communication Workers Union (CWU) and Unite the Union. The unions are framing this as the world's first union at a frontier-AI lab. Stated grievances center on Google's Pentagon contract signed the prior Friday (1 May, the same agreement that excluded Anthropic from seven companion vendor awards) and Google's earlier decision to drop its public pledge against weapons-related AI. Workers gave management 10 working days to voluntarily recognize the unions or agree to mediated negotiations before triggering a formal statutory recognition process. Demands include reinstating the no-weapons / no-mass-surveillance pledge, an independent ethics oversight body, and an individual right to refuse work on moral grounds. Workers have separately threatened "research strikes" that would touch core Gemini products.
