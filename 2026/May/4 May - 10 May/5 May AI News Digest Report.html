Same-day, mirror-image launches by the two frontier labs underscore the new theory of the case: at parity-class capabilities, distribution and integration is the moat.

White HouseTrump administration drafting executive order to vet AI models pre-release (NYT)

4 May 2026 · Primary source: The New York Times (paywalled); Bloomberg / Reuters wires; U.S. News & World Report

The New York Times reported on 4 May that the Trump White House is drafting an executive order that would establish a working group of tech executives and government officials to design a pre-release review process for new frontier AI models — a sharp reversal of the administration's hands-off line. NYT says White House staff briefed Anthropic, Google, and OpenAI executives on the proposal in meetings last week. A White House official told reporters "any policy announcement will come directly from the president" and called the executive-order discussion "speculation," but did not deny the underlying conversations. The signal — if the order materialises — would mark the first U.S. government move toward something resembling pre-deployment safety evals at a federal level, just as the EU AI Act's 2 August 2026 high-risk obligations head to a 13 May trilogue that may defer them to December 2027.
