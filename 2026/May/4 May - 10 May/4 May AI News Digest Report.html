MicrosoftAgent 365 hits general availability; M365 E7 "Frontier Suite" launches

1 May 2026 · Primary source: Microsoft Security Blog / Microsoft Learn

Satya Nadella announced general availability of Microsoft Agent 365, positioning it as the enterprise control plane for the post-Copilot agent sprawl. Agent 365 gives admins a single registry of every first-party and third-party agent, with identity, security, governance, and lifecycle management extended from users to bots — including registry sync into AWS Bedrock and Google Cloud agent runtimes. Pricing is $15 per user / month stand-alone, or bundled with Microsoft 365 E5 + Copilot into the new M365 E7 "Frontier Suite" at roughly $99/user/month. Launch services partners include Accenture, Bechtle, Capgemini, Insight, KPMG, Protiviti, and Slalom. Agent 365 ships at exactly the moment enterprises are admitting they have hundreds of unmanaged agents on the network.

Frontier Research & Model Releases

Mistral AIMedium 3.5 ships as a 128 B dense open-weight frontier coder

29 April 2026 · Primary source: Mistral AI / Mistral Docs

Mistral released Mistral Medium 3.5, a 128 B-parameter dense (non-MoE) transformer with a 256 K-token context window and a from-scratch native vision encoder, under a modified MIT license. The release collapses Mistral's three previous lines — Medium 3.1 (general), Magistral (reasoning), and Devstral 2 (agentic coding) — into a single model. Company-reported benchmarks: 77.6% on SWE-Bench Verified and 91.4 on τ³-Telecom, ahead of Devstral 2 and within shouting distance of Qwen3.5 397B at roughly a third of the active parameter count. Pricing through the API is $1.50 in / $7.50 out per million tokens, and Mistral claims the model self-hosts on as few as four H100 / H200 GPUs — a deliberate pitch to EU enterprises that want sovereign, on-prem frontier capability ahead of the August 2026 AI Act deadline.

Geopolitics & Industry Strategy

U.S. DoWPentagon awards classified-network AI contracts to seven; Anthropic excluded

1 May 2026 · Primary source: U.S. Department of War (formerly DoD)

The renamed U.S. Department of War announced classified-network AI procurement deals with OpenAI, Google, Microsoft, Amazon Web Services, Nvidia, SpaceX, and Reflection AI — covering deployment at Impact Level 6 and 7 (Secret and above) inside the GenAI.mil portal, which now serves more than 1.3 million DoW users. Conspicuously absent: Anthropic, which was frozen out after refusing to allow Claude to be used for "all lawful purposes" — language Anthropic argued could enable domestic mass surveillance or fully autonomous weapons. The exclusion formalizes a supply-chain-risk designation Defense Secretary Pete Hegseth signed off on in March, and lands while Anthropic's parallel federal lawsuit against the administration remains live (a California judge blocked an earlier government action last month). Reflection AI, the only newcomer on the list, is the ex-DeepMind venture that raised $2 B in 2025.

MetaMeta acquires Assured Robot Intelligence into Superintelligence Labs

1 May 2026 · Primary source: Bloomberg / TechCrunch

Meta closed its acquisition of Assured Robot Intelligence, a stealth-stage robot-foundation-model startup co-founded by NYU's Lerrel Pinto and UC San Diego's Xiaolong Wang (ex-Nvidia). Terms weren't disclosed. The team folds into Meta Superintelligence Labs to work on whole-body humanoid control, model-based self-learning, and the underlying perception stack for Meta's in-house humanoid hardware program. Pinto previously co-founded Fauna Robotics — which Amazon scooped up in March — and the deal effectively neutralizes one of the last remaining independent humanoid-AI labs in the U.S. academic ecosystem. It's Meta's clearest signal yet that humanoids are now a Mark-Zuckerberg-personal-priority bet alongside the Llama family.

Markets, Capital & Infrastructure

KKRHelix Digital Infrastructure launches with $10 B+ and ex-AWS CEO Adam Selipsky as chair

30 April 2026 · Primary source: Bloomberg / Helix Digital Infrastructure

KKR publicly launched Helix Digital Infrastructure, a new vehicle that has secured more than $10 B in equity commitments — including from at least one sovereign wealth fund and two strategic partners — to develop and operate AI infrastructure end-to-end: data centers, on-site power generation, transmission, fiber, and cooling. Adam Selipsky, who ran AWS through its hyper-growth phase, signs on as CEO and chair. The pitch is that hyperscalers no longer want AI build-out on their balance sheets; Helix takes the capex, the permitting risk, and the multi-year delivery schedule, and locks the hyperscaler in via long-dated capacity contracts. It's the most concrete signal yet that the 2026 AI capex cycle is now being financed off-balance-sheet through purpose-built private vehicles, not directly by the hyperscalers themselves.

NebiusNebius buys Eigen AI for $643 M to harden Token Factory inference

1 May 2026 · Primary source: Nebius Newsroom / BusinessWire

Nebius Group agreed to acquire Eigen AI, a 20-person MIT HAN-Lab spinout, for roughly $643 M — about $98 M cash plus 3.8 M Class A shares, with founders' equity vesting over four years. Eigen was founded by Ryan Hanrui Wang (author of Sparse Attention / SpAtten, the most-cited HPCA paper since 2020) and Wei-Chen Wang (MLSys 2024 Best Paper for AWQ, activation-aware weight quantization). The team's full-stack inference-optimization work — covering post-training, fine-tuning, and production serving across GPT-OSS, Gemma, Qwen, Llama, Nemotron, DeepSeek, GLM, Kimi, and MiniMax — folds into Nebius Token Factory. The transaction underscores a broader thesis (loud across this week's deal flow): inference optimization, not training scale, is now the most valuable layer of the AI infra stack.
