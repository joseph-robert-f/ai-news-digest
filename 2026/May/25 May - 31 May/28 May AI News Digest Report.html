OpenAIAltman in Sydney: "I'm delighted to be wrong" on the AI jobs apocalypse

26–28 May 2026 · Primary source: Sam Altman fireside with CBA CEO Matt Comyn (Sydney) · Reuters / Euronews / TechRadar

Speaking on stage at Commonwealth Bank of Australia's Sydney conference on Tuesday 26 May, OpenAI CEO Sam Altman explicitly walked back his earlier predictions that AI would compress decades of job turnover into a few years. Altman told CBA chief executive Matt Comyn he was "roughly right" on the technological trajectory after ChatGPT's 2022 launch but "pretty wrong" on the social and economic impact: "I thought there would have been more impact on entry-level white-collar jobs being eliminated by now than has actually happened. I'm delighted to be wrong about this." He cited his own habit of writing personal replies to email and Slack messages he had earlier started outsourcing to ChatGPT — "we really do care about people" — as anecdotal evidence that human-to-human work is more sticky than the labor-displacement models predicted. The comments are striking in context: in 2024–2025 Altman repeatedly forecast that AI could compress the historical 50%-of-jobs-changing-every-75-years rate into a much shorter window. Goldman Sachs CEO David Solomon made a similar pivot the same week. The timing — six days after OpenAI's confidential S-1 filing — makes this the first material softening of OpenAI's public narrative on labor-market disruption ahead of a $1 T listing.

EUAI Act “Omnibus”: transparency grace period halved to 3 months; sandbox deadline slipped to August 2027

7 May agreement · In-force impact 26–28 May · Primary source: Council of the EU press release / European Commission

Following the political agreement reached on 7 May 2026 between the European Council and Parliament on the so-called AI Omnibus, the practical compliance calendar for AI providers operating in the EU has now been locked in for the back half of 2026 and is shaping commercial planning this week. Two changes are material: (1) the grace period for transparency obligations on AI-generated content is being cut from six months to three, with the binding date now 2 December 2026; and (2) the deadline for national authorities to stand up AI regulatory sandboxes has slipped to 2 August 2027. Prohibited practices and GPAI-model rules remain enforceable today. High-risk AI obligations continue to take effect on 2 August 2026, subject to the Omnibus amendments still being finalized. Fines remain capped at €35 M or 7% of global annual turnover. The practical implication for product teams shipping to the EU: any model-generated-content provenance and watermarking work has lost three months of runway, and US-based startups still treating the AI Act as a 2027 problem are now mechanically late.
