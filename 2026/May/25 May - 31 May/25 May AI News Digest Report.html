The unusual pairing — a sitting pope and one of the most influential mechanistic-interpretability researchers in the field — signals that the Vatican has chosen AI safety and human-centric governance, rather than a categorical refusal, as its theological frame for the coming decade.

OpenAIInternal reasoning model autonomously disproves the Erdős unit-distance conjecture

20–21 May 2026 · Primary source: OpenAI / GIGAZINE

OpenAI disclosed that one of its general-purpose reasoning models, given only the problem statement, produced a new construction that disproves a central conjecture in discrete geometry posed by Paul Erdős in 1946. The unit-distance problem asks: given n points in the plane, what is the maximum number of pairs exactly distance 1 apart? The classical conjecture said the answer was essentially the square-grid bound. OpenAI's model produced an infinite family of constructions using Golod–Shafarevich theory and infinite class-field towers, beating the square grid by a polynomial factor of n1+δ. Princeton's Will Sawin subsequently sharpened the result to δ ≈ 0.014. Fields medalist Tim Gowers called the result "a milestone in AI mathematics"; Noga Alon described the solution as "an outstanding achievement." Importantly, the model was not steered step-by-step and did not complete a partial human proof — this is the first time AI has resolved a prominent open problem central to a subfield of mathematics on its own.

AnthropicAcquires Stainless for $300 M+, ending hosted SDK generation used by OpenAI, Google and Cloudflare

18 May 2026 · Primary source: Anthropic Newsroom / TechCrunch

Anthropic acquired Stainless, the SDK-generation startup whose tooling has produced and maintained the official SDKs of OpenAI, Google, and Cloudflare (among many others) since 2022. Reported price: $300 M+. Stainless's value prop was simple but load-bearing for the AI industry: take an OpenAPI / spec change, regenerate idiomatic SDKs across TypeScript, Python, Go, Java and others, and keep them current as APIs evolve. Anthropic confirmed it will wind down all hosted Stainless products, including the SDK generator. Existing customers retain ownership and modification rights over the SDKs already generated, but new SDK generation will be limited to Anthropic going forward — meaning OpenAI, Google, Cloudflare and the rest now have to rebuild or migrate the toolchain that ships their developer surface. Strategically this hands Anthropic a structural advantage on agent connectivity, since SDKs are the entry point through which agents call third-party APIs.
