OpenAIOpenAI Deployment Company launches with $4 B, acquires Tomoro

11 May 2026 · Primary source: OpenAI

OpenAI announced the OpenAI Deployment Company, a majority-owned, OpenAI-controlled subsidiary whose sole job is to embed Forward Deployed Engineers (FDEs) into enterprises building production AI. The launch is anchored by $4 B+ in committed capital at a roughly $14 B valuation, with TPG, Advent, Bain Capital, and Brookfield as co-lead founding partners and B Capital, BBVA, Emergence, Goanna, Goldman Sachs, SoftBank, Warburg Pincus, and WCAS as founding partners. The structural read: OpenAI is formally separating an enterprise-services business that competes head-on with Accenture's Anthropic-aligned practice and Palantir's FDE model, while keeping the IP under OpenAI's roof.

AI Security & Governance

GoogleGTIG catches the first confirmed AI-built zero-day in the wild

11 May 2026 · Primary source: Google Cloud Threat Intelligence

Google Threat Intelligence Group (GTIG) published the first public case of a threat actor deploying an AI-generated zero-day exploit. The vulnerability — a 2FA bypass in a widely deployed open-source, web-based system-administration tool — was identified inside a Python script that GTIG says was almost certainly built with an LLM: "an abundance of educational docstrings, including a hallucinated CVSS score, and uses a structured, textbook Pythonic format highly characteristic of LLMs training data." The actors were staging it for a mass exploitation event; GTIG says proactive disclosure to the maintainer disrupted the campaign. GTIG explicitly notes the exploit was not built with Gemini, and separately reports continued criminal experimentation with agentic offensive tools branded OpenClaw and OneClaw. The report lands one week after CISA, NCSC-UK, and the German BSI issued joint guidance on AI-augmented vulnerability research — and reframes the "AI for offensive security" debate from theoretical to operational.

EUBrussels gets GPT-5.5-Cyber preview; Anthropic still withholds Mythos

11 May 2026 · Primary source: European Commission briefing / OpenAI

The European Commission confirmed it has secured a limited-preview deal with OpenAI giving vetted EU cybersecurity teams, national authorities, and the EU AI Office access to GPT-5.5-Cyber, the offensive-security-tuned variant of GPT-5.5 that OpenAI rolled out to a small set of U.S. defenders last week. The Commission says it has held "four or five" meetings with Anthropic but is "not yet at the same stage" on Mythos, which Anthropic still distributes only to ~40 vetted U.S. firms (Apple, Amazon, JPMorgan, Palo Alto Networks, etc.) under its Project Glasswing framework. The asymmetry is becoming a diplomatic flashpoint: the U.K. AI Security Institute's most recent scorecard had Mythos at 3/10 and GPT-5.5 at 2/10 on a 32-step end-to-end red-team range, so the EU is effectively settling for the second-best frontier cyber model because it can actually get it.

Helsing, the Munich-based AI-defense-software-turned-drone-manufacturer founded in 2021, is closing a $1.2 B round at an $18 B valuation — up roughly 30% in dollar terms from the €12 B mark it hit in June 2025. The combined news cements Helsing as the most valuable defense-tech company on the continent and an unmistakable signal that European LPs are now writing growth-equity checks into autonomous-weapons platforms.
