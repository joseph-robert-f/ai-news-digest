14 May 2026 · Primary source: OpenAI

OpenAI shipped Codex inside the ChatGPT mobile app on iOS and Android, framing the release as "work with Codex from anywhere." The phone is positioned as a thin control surface: connect to any machine where Codex is running — a laptop, a dedicated Mac mini, or a managed remote environment — and the app loads live state from that environment, so users can move between active threads, approvals, plugins, and project context without breaking flow. Notion launched a Developer Platform that recasts the workspace as an agent control room. AI Research & Benchmarks

arXivTwo new agent benchmarks land: GroupMemBench (multi-party memory) and Cattle Trade (bluffing & bargaining)

14 May 2026 · Primary source: arXiv

Two complementary agent benchmarks dropped on arXiv on 14 May, both targeting capabilities that 2026 production agents still demonstrably under-deliver. GroupMemBench (arXiv:2605.14498) is the first benchmark to score LLM agents on multi-party memory: most existing memory systems are tuned for dyadic, single-user chats, but real deployments span groups and channels with several speakers. The benchmark scores three properties — group dynamics beyond concatenated one-on-ones, speaker-grounded belief tracking, and audience-adapted language (Theory-of-Mind shifts that produce role-specific vocabulary) — using a graph-grounded synthesis pipeline. Cattle Trade (arXiv:2605.14537) is a long-horizon, multi-agent economic game (auctions, hidden-offer trade, bargaining, bluffing, opponent modeling) that runs 50–60 turns; the authors evaluated seven cost-efficient LLMs and three deterministic code agents across 242 games, finding that strategic coherence — spending discipline and phase-adaptive bidding — correlates with rank far more than spending volume or any single sub-skill. Together they sharpen the open question heading into Google I/O: are the next-gen "omni" models actually better collaborators and strategists, or just better single-shot generators?

GridCARE$64 M Series A creates "Power Acceleration for AI" category; Sutter Hill leads

14–15 May 2026 · Primary source: GridCARE / BusinessWire

GridCARE, a Stanford Doerr School of Sustainability spin-out, closed an oversubscribed $64 M Series A led by Sutter Hill Ventures (early NVIDIA, Snowflake, Astera Labs) at "a significant step-up in valuation" from its seed less than a year ago. Its Energize platform applies physics-based AI to live grid topology to identify and activate near-term stranded capacity — the constraint that has become the binding limit on new AI data centers in PJM, ERCOT, and CAISO. GridCARE says it has already unlocked more than $10 B in economic value for developers by bringing hundreds of MW online years ahead of standard interconnect timelines, and is now engaged on projects spanning more than a dozen markets and 2 GW of new AI compute capacity. The company is using the round to define a new category — Power Acceleration for AI — and to host an inaugural summit in September 2026. The round dovetails with this week's Anthropic / Akamai $1.8 B compute deal: the bottleneck conversation has officially shifted from chips to electrons.
