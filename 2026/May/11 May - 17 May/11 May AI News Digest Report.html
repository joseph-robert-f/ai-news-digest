OpenAIGPT-5.5 Instant ships as ChatGPT's new default model

5 May 2026 · Primary source: OpenAI

OpenAI made GPT-5.5 Instant the default model in ChatGPT, fully replacing GPT-5.3 Instant for all users and the chat-latest alias in the API. The headline numbers from OpenAI's internal evaluations: 52.5% fewer hallucinated claims than GPT-5.3 Instant on high-stakes prompts (medicine, law, finance), 37.3% fewer inaccurate claims on flagged factual-error conversations, and outputs that are 30.2% shorter by word count and 29.2% shorter by line count — a deliberate “less filler, more workplace-safe” trim. Enhanced personalisation is initially Plus/Pro on web only; Free, Go, Business and Enterprise will follow. GPT-5.3 Instant remains available to paid API users for three months before retirement.

NIST / CAISIPre-deployment evaluation pacts signed with Google DeepMind, Microsoft and xAI

5 May 2026 · Primary source: NIST CAISI / Microsoft On the Issues

NIST's Center for AI Standards and Innovation (CAISI) announced expanded collaborations with Google DeepMind, Microsoft and xAI to perform pre-deployment evaluations and targeted national-security research on frontier AI models — including in classified environments and on models with safeguards reduced or removed, to better characterise unmitigated capabilities. Google DeepMindUK staff vote 98% to unionise — first union at any frontier AI lab

5–6 May 2026 · Primary source: Communication Workers Union

DeepMind's UK staff, organising through the Communication Workers Union (CWU) and Unite the Union, voted 98% in favour of forming a union — the first union recognition campaign at a frontier AI lab. The trigger: Google's classified contract allowing the U.S. Department of Defense to use Gemini inside military networks “for any lawful purpose,” which staff argue overrides DeepMind's eight-year-old ethics pledges and opens the door to autonomous-weapon and mass-surveillance use. Workers are also pushing to terminate Google AI use by the Israeli military. The CWU has formally written to Google requesting recognition. Notably, of the Pentagon's recent Big-Tech AI deals (Google, OpenAI, xAI, NVIDIA, Microsoft, Amazon), only Anthropic declined — and was labelled a “supply-chain risk.”
