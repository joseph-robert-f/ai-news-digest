DeepSeek-AI“Thinking with Visual Primitives”: points and bounding boxes as first-class units of MLLM thought

30 April 2026 · Primary source: arXiv (DeepSeek-AI)

DeepSeek-AI released a research preprint Thursday introducing a multimodal-reasoning framework that promotes points and bounding boxes from being mere outputs to being fundamental tokens of intermediate reasoning inside Multimodal LLMs. The motivation is what the authors call the “Reference Gap”: standard MLLMs can describe scenes but struggle to refer back to specific image regions during chain-of-thought, which makes complex visual reasoning — counting, occlusion, fine-grained spatial relations — brittle. By treating geometric primitives as “thoughts” that can be emitted, attended to, and chained, the proposed model substantially improves on standard visual-reasoning benchmarks. Pair this with the GLM-V Team's GLM-5V-Turbo release on 29 April — which integrates visual perception directly into agentic reasoning, planning, and tool-use — and the “visual primitives as compute” thesis is now a credible contender for the next post-text reasoning paradigm.

Cognition CritiqueZhejiang University: Centaur isn't simulating cognition — it's overfitting

29 April 2026 · Primary source: National Science Open

A new critique by Wei Liu and Nai Ding (Zhejiang University), now circulating in the English-language press following an April 29 ScienceDaily push, takes apart the Centaur “foundation model of human cognition” (Nature, 2025) that claimed to mimic human responses across 160 cognitive tasks. The authors' core attack is a prompt-substitution test: they replace the original task prompts with a single instruction — “Please choose option A” — and find Centaur still emits the “correct” original-dataset answers, which is only consistent with the model having memorized response patterns rather than internalized task structure. The paper, published in National Science Open, argues Centaur's apparent cognitive generality is largely distribution-fit overfitting to the Psych-101 corpus and that genuine human-like reasoning would require demonstrating intent recognition, not just answer reproduction. The episode is a useful counterweight to the “LLMs as cognitive models” narrative that has gained ground in computational neuroscience.
