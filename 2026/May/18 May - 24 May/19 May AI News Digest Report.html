Musk v. AltmanJury rejects Musk's $134 B suit against OpenAI on statute of limitations after < 2 hours of deliberation

18 May 2026 · Primary source: U.S. District Court, N.D. Cal. / Washington Post / NPR

A nine-member federal advisory jury in Oakland deliberated for less than two hours before unanimously finding that Elon Musk filed his 2024 suit against Sam Altman and OpenAI outside California's three-year statute of limitations. Judge Yvonne Gonzalez Rogers agreed and dismissed the case from the bench. The suit, originally seeking up to $134–150 B in disgorgement against OpenAI and Microsoft, alleged that Altman and Greg Brockman breached an early agreement to operate OpenAI as a charitable nonprofit. The jury did not reach the merits of the breach-of-charitable-trust claim. Musk called the verdict a "calendar technicality" on X and vowed to appeal to the Ninth Circuit; OpenAI said the decision "fully vindicates" its restructuring. The ruling effectively clears the largest remaining legal cloud over OpenAI's PBC conversion.

EU AI ActDigital Omnibus on AI: 16-month extension for high-risk providers; new EU-wide nudification ban from December

18 May 2026 · Primary source: European Commission / Council of the EU

The political deal known as the Digital Omnibus on AI, agreed by Parliament and Council on 7 May and worked into implementing text over the past week, has been distilled by EU regulators in fresh guidance issued late on 18 May. Headline changes: Annex III high-risk obligations (employment, credit-scoring, education, certain healthcare uses) shift from 2 August 2026 to 2 December 2027; high-risk AI embedded in regulated Annex I products gets until 2 August 2028; and watermarking obligations for generative-AI systems already on the market before 2 August 2026 defer to 2 December 2026. The package also introduces a new EU-wide prohibition on AI-generated non-consensual intimate imagery from 2 December 2026 — a direct response to the Grok image-generation scandal that produced an estimated 3 M sexualized images in 11 days. Formal adoption is expected by late June, with entry into force ahead of 2 August.

VS Code1.118 ships: remote control for Copilot CLI, semantic codebase search — and a contentious Git co-author default

18 May 2026 · Primary source: Visual Studio Code Release Notes

Microsoft published the VS Code 1.118 stable release notes, the largest agent-focused update of the cycle. New capabilities: remote control of Copilot CLI sessions from GitHub.com or the GitHub mobile app, semantic codebase search across any workspace and across GitHub repos/orgs, chat-history insights that auto-summarize work into standup-ready reports, expanded enterprise allow-listing of AI features, and token-efficiency improvements ahead of GitHub Copilot's usage-based billing switch on 1 June 2026. The Claude Code agent now ships natively in the Agents app, runnable alongside Copilot CLI and Copilot Cloud. The release also quietly enabled Copilot as a Git commit co-author by default for chat and agent workflows — a change drawing significant pushback from open-source maintainers; Microsoft has signaled it will reconsider the default in a follow-up release.
