White HouseTrump postpones AI cybersecurity executive order, citing China-race concerns

21 May 2026 · Primary source: White House remarks (via pool) / Bloomberg / WaPo

Hours before a planned Thursday-afternoon Oval Office signing — with invitations already out to Big-Tech executives — President Trump pulled back the executive order that would have empowered the federal government to pre-evaluate frontier AI models for security vulnerabilities (with NSA participation in classified red-teaming) under a voluntary 90-day pre-launch review framework. Trump told reporters he "didn't like certain aspects of it" and said the U.S. lead over China meant he didn't want anything that "could have been a blocker." The order had been in interagency review since March; staff are now expected to redraft, but no replacement timeline was given. The pullback is the single largest U.S. AI-policy signal of Q2 and effectively kills (for now) the only formal federal evaluation regime for frontier models.

GoogleAntigravity 2.0 ships as agent-first desktop+CLI+SDK; AI Pro/Ultra plans gate access

19–21 May 2026 · Primary source: Google Developers Blog (I/O 2026)

At I/O 2026 Google detached Antigravity from the editor and shipped Antigravity 2.0 as a standalone desktop application (macOS / Windows / Linux) with a new Go-based CLI, an SDK for custom agents, and Managed Agents in the Gemini API. The UX is deliberately not an IDE: a chat window orchestrates parallel coding agents that plan, write, run tests, and reconcile diffs across sub-projects, with a headline demo of a parallel agent fleet booting a from-scratch OS kernel and running a Doom clone on top of it for under $1,000 in tokens. Access requires the new $100/month AI Ultra plan (Pro tier capped at lower parallelism). Google also confirmed that the legacy Gemini CLI and Gemini Code Assist IDE extensions will be sunset on 18 June 2026 for AI Pro, AI Ultra, and free-tier users — a forcing function that has already triggered visible developer pushback.
