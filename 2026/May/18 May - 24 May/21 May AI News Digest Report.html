VS Code 1.121Built-in remote agent sessions and a new open Agent Host Protocol (AHP)

20 May 2026 · Primary source: code.visualstudio.com

Microsoft shipped Visual Studio Code 1.121 on 20 May, and the headline is structural: the Agents window can now monitor and steer long-lived agent sessions running on a remote machine you own, connected via SSH or dev tunnels — the remote agent host keeps running even when the local client disconnects. The wire format is a brand-new Agent Host Protocol (AHP), developed in the open as a standalone spec and explicitly designed to let multiple clients coordinate against the same agent session. Smaller but consequential: model configurability for lightweight tasks (commit messages, titles, etc.), built-in Mermaid and HTML previews inside Markdown/notebooks, and terminal-tool optimizations that compress output and clean up idle background terminals to reduce token spend on agentic loops. AHP is the second major open agent-host spec to ship this quarter (Anthropic's MCP remains the de-facto tool-calling layer), and the editor wars are now visibly an agent-orchestration war.

FTCTAKE IT DOWN Act enforcement live: $53,088-per-violation penalties for missed 48-hour deepfake takedowns

19 May 2026 · Primary source: FTC Consumer Advice

Exactly one year after President Trump signed it, the federal TAKE IT DOWN Act (TiDA) reached its operational milestone on 19 May 2026. From that date, the FTC can begin imposing civil penalties of $53,088 per violation on covered platforms — public sites and online services hosting user-generated content — that fail to remove non-consensual intimate images, including AI-generated deepfakes, within 48 hours of a properly noticed request from the depicted person. The criminal prohibition has already produced its first conviction (an Ohio defendant, April 2026). Compliance fallout: model providers and platforms are urgently revisiting takedown SLAs, notice intake pipelines, hash-matching against known copies, and image classifiers tuned for non-consensual intimate imagery. For developers shipping generative-image features inside consumer apps, TiDA is now a hard constraint, not a future risk.
