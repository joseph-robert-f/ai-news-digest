Google I/O 2026 — Frontier Models & Agents

Google DeepMindGemini 3.5 family launches: Flash, Omni (video), Spark (background agent)

19 May 2026 · Primary source: Google blog / Google Developers Blog

Google opened I/O with the Gemini 3.5 series. 3.5 Flash is the new default across Search and the API, marketed as "frontier intelligence at Flash speed and price"—Google's own numbers show it leading MCP Atlas at 83.6% (vs. Claude Opus 4.7 79.1% and GPT-5.5 75.3%), Terminal-Bench 2.1 at 76.2%, and roughly 4× the throughput of comparable frontier models. Gemini Omni is a unified video model that can re-edit existing footage from a text prompt (adding or removing characters/objects) and ships into Flash, the Gemini app, Google Flow, and YouTube Shorts. Gemini Spark is a new general-purpose background agent inside the Gemini app that can reason across connected services and keep tasks running asynchronously; it rolls out to AI Ultra subscribers and trusted testers starting next week. Google also cut the Ultra subscription from $250 to $200/month and introduced a $100/month Developer tier.

Google I/O 2026 — Developer Platforms

GoogleAntigravity 2.0 ships: standalone desktop, CLI, SDK, managed-execution — replaces Gemini CLI

19 May 2026 · Primary source: Google Developers Blog

Google relaunched Antigravity as a standalone, agent-first developer platform spanning a desktop app for orchestrating parallel agents, an Antigravity CLI (Go-based, "snappier and more responsive"), an Antigravity SDK for hosting custom agents on your own infra, Managed Agents in the Gemini API, and enterprise governance via the Gemini Enterprise Agent Platform. New surface features include dynamic subagents for parallel workflows, scheduled tasks for background automation, and first-party integrations with AI Studio, Android, and Firebase. The kicker for developers: Gemini CLI is being sunset—on 18 June 2026, Gemini CLI and Gemini Code Assist IDE extensions stop serving requests for AI Pro, Ultra, and free users (Standard/Enterprise Code Assist licenses keep working). Agent Skills, Hooks, Subagents, and Extensions carry forward as Antigravity plugins.

GoogleAI Studio gains native Kotlin: prompt → production Android app, with ADB sideload and Play Internal Test publishing

19 May 2026 · Primary source: Google blog / Android Developers Blog

Google AI Studio picked up first-class Android support: it now generates production-quality Kotlin code using current Jetpack Compose patterns, previews the app in an in-browser Android Emulator, sideloads to a real device via ADB, and (with a connected Google Play Developer account) publishes directly to the Play Internal Test Track in one click. Android Studio separately previewed an agent that migrates existing apps to native Kotlin—turning multi-week refactors into hours—and Google released Android Bench, a new LLM leaderboard for Android dev tasks that includes open-weight models like Gemma 4. The combined effect: AI Studio is now a credible end-to-end mobile build surface, not just a prompt sandbox.

Google I/O 2026 — Search & XR Hardware

Google SearchBiggest search-box overhaul in ~25 years: AI Mode default, persistent "information agents," 1 B MAU

19 May 2026 · Primary source: Google blog (Search)

Google rebuilt the Search query box around AI. The field now expands to accept long, conversational prompts and surfaces guided suggestions that go well beyond autocomplete. Gemini 3.5 Flash becomes the default model in AI Mode for all users globally, and starting this summer users can spawn personal "information agents" inside Search—persistent, named jobs (apartment hunts, apparel drops, regulatory filings, etc.) that watch the web 24/7 and notify on change. Google said AI Mode just crossed 1 B monthly active users, with query volume "more than doubling every quarter" since launch. The shift formalises Search's pivot from the ten blue links to an agent entry point.

Samsung + GoogleFirst Android XR smart glasses unveiled: audio-only and microdisplay SKUs, fall 2026 launch

19 May 2026 · Primary source: Google I/O keynote / Engadget

Google and Samsung publicly demoed the first wearable to run Android XR: a Gemini-powered smart-glasses platform with two SKUs—an audio-only variant (cameras, mic, speakers, no screen) and a screened variant with a small in-lens microdisplay for turn-by-turn navigation, message previews, and contextual overlays. Talent, Capital & Workforce

Google DeepMind~$90 M license + 20-person acquihire of Bezos-backed Contextual AI, including CEO Douwe Kiela

19 May 2026 · Primary source: Bloomberg / Reuters

Google DeepMind is hiring more than 20 researchers from Bezos-backed Contextual AI and licensing parts of its tech stack in a deal reportedly valued at $80–90 M (one outlet pegs it near $100 M). Contextual AI's co-founder and CEO, Douwe Kiela—widely credited as a pioneer of retrieval-augmented generation—is among those joining DeepMind. Critically, Google is not acquiring Contextual AI as a company: by structuring this as a license-plus-acquihire (the same pattern Google used for Hume, Character.AI, and Windsurf, and that Microsoft used for Inflection), the transaction sidesteps U.S. antitrust pre-clearance. The deal lands the same day Google launched Gemini 3.5 with a heavy RAG/agentic-retrieval pitch, and signals continued consolidation of frontier-RAG talent into the hyperscalers.

Meta~8,000 layoffs begin today; 6,000 open reqs cancelled, survivors funnelled into new AI org

20 May 2026 · Primary source: Meta internal memo via Variety / TechCrunch reporting

Meta started notifying roughly 8,000 employees of layoffs on Wednesday, 20 May, the company's largest single-day cut since 2023 and equivalent to ~10% of headcount. Singapore staff received notices first (4 a.m. local), followed by U.K. and U.S. rollouts through the day. CHRO Janelle Gale's memo says the company is also cancelling ~6,000 open requisitions, taking the effective headcount reduction to ~14,000 roles, and that ~7,000 redeployed employees will move into new AI-centric orgs including Applied AI Engineering, Agent Transformation Accelerator XFN, and Central Analytics. CEO Mark Zuckerberg framed the move as necessary to free room for $125–145 B in 2026 capex (vs. $72.2 B in 2025), with additional cuts signposted for H2 2026. The cuts land while Meta's quarterly revenue is at a record $56 B—explicitly a reallocation, not a downturn.
