Top Story — Amodei's Policy Turn

AnthropicDario Amodei publishes “Policy on the AI Exponential”: FAA-style pre-release safety testing, $350 M in economic-impact funding

10 June 2026 · Primary source: darioamodei.com / Anthropic policy framework / Axios

Anthropic CEO Dario Amodei on Wednesday published a sweeping policy essay on his personal site, “Policy on the AI Exponential,” covering five areas: safety regulation, economics and taxes, science acceleration, civil liberties, and geopolitics. The headline proposal is a deliberate move past Anthropic's earlier “transparency-only” posture: Amodei argues that “the rapid pace of acceleration means that transparency alone is no longer sufficient” and that governments need a more substantial role. His replacement model is the U.S. Federal Aviation Administration: “Frontier AI models, like airplanes, should be required to go through technical testing and auditing, and their release should be blocked or reversed as a threat to public safety if they do not meet high standards of safety.” On the labor side, Amodei concedes “it's reasonable to think that AI could produce much larger disruptions to the labor market than previous technologies, and, potentially, more enduring disruptions,” and calls for wage insurance, retention tax incentives, and consideration of universal basic income or universal capital accounts. Anthropic operationalized the essay with a paired financial commitment: a $200 M Economic Futures Research Fund to back research trials and program evaluation of public-policy responses, plus a $150 M national fellowship for early-career professionals (totaling $350 M). The policy framework sets recommendations against three escalating economic scenarios — 5%, 10%, and an unspecified “unprecedented” national unemployment rate. The combined effect of essay and dollar commitments is the clearest signal yet that the frontier-lab side of the policy conversation is shifting from “please don't over-regulate” toward “please regulate, here is the schema.”

Public Sentiment & Policy Signals

PollingReuters/Ipsos: 64% of Americans disapprove of the AI data-center build pace; 77% expect higher electricity bills

11 June 2026 · Primary source: Reuters / Ipsos

A Reuters/Ipsos poll of 4,531 Americans closed Monday 8 June and published Wednesday 11 June lays out the sharpest gap to date between AI-cohort capital formation and public sentiment. Only 33% of respondents agreed that building data centers at the current rapid pace is “mainly a good thing,” while 64% said it was “mainly a bad thing”; 57% — including two-thirds of Democrats and half of Republicans — said they would actively oppose a data center in their own community, and only 14% said they were comfortable with one being built near them. Most strikingly, 77% of respondents — with similar shares of Republicans, Democrats, and independents — said they were worried that AI would raise their electricity prices, a near-universal cross-partisan signal. The poll context: there are now 710 data centers operating in the U.S. with another 1,062 planned, and the November 3 midterms are about five months out. The poll lands the same week as the Department of Energy's confirmation that it will open four DOE-controlled federal sites — Idaho National Laboratory, Oak Ridge Reservation, Paducah, and Savannah River — to private AI data-center development under President Trump's executive orders. The combination of NIMBY opposition, electricity-cost concern, and federal-land siting authority will shape AI-infrastructure policy through the back half of 2026.

EUFinal Code of Practice on AI-generated content transparency expected this month; soft sign-by deadline 22 July

Ongoing this week · Primary source: European Commission / AI Office

Distinct from the AI-Act Omnibus calendar covered in the 28 May digest, the European Commission's Code of Practice on Transparency of AI-Generated Content is in its final-publication window this month. The Code is the practical companion to Article 50 of the AI Act (transparency obligations for generative-AI providers and deployers) and creates a “presumption of conformity” safe harbor for signatories. The AI Office is encouraging providers and deployers to sign by 22 July 2026, and Article 50(2), (4), and (5) become formally applicable on 2 August 2026. After that date, a provider that has not signed will need to demonstrate compliance with the labeling-and-marking obligations through other means — meaning the cost of not signing is meaningfully higher than the cost of signing. Combined with the Omnibus — which already shortened the transparency grace period to three months (binding 2 December 2026) — product teams shipping any generative-content surface into the EU now have a hard six-week window to make a signing decision.
