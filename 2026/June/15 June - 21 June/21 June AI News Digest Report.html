Top Story — The Transformer's Co-Architect Switches Labs

OpenAI / GoogleNoam Shazeer leaves Google DeepMind for OpenAI as Lead for Architecture Research

18 June 2026 · Primary sources: Axios scoop · X (Shazeer post) · confirmed across Fast Company, MLQ, Silicon Republic, IBT

Shortly after midnight Pacific on Thursday 18 June, Noam Shazeer posted on X that he is leaving Google DeepMind to join OpenAI as Lead for Architecture Research, where he will "explore next-generation model architectures and drive further evolution of the transformer." Axios's Ina Fried broke the news, and Google confirmed within hours. The move is, by any reasonable yard-stick, the single largest individual-researcher shift since Karpathy's May move to Anthropic. Shazeer co-authored the seminal Attention Is All You Need paper that introduced the transformer in 2017, co-invented multi-query attention, co-led the Mesh-TensorFlow and Switch Transformer work that underpins today's MoE serving stacks, co-founded Character.AI, and, after Google's $2.7 B reverse-acquihire in August 2024 brought him back, has been one of the two named co-leads of Gemini — including the Gemini 3.x family and the Gemini Omni multimodal video stack that shipped on Tuesday. His departure lands one week before the self-imposed end-of-June launch window for Gemini 3.5 Pro, and pulls the specific person most responsible for the architectural choices behind that family across the table to OpenAI's stack. The competitive read for builders: OpenAI's pre-IPO quiet period just gained a marquee technical hire whose presence will be visible in the next post-IPO architecture release; Google's competitive moat around the Gemini family is now meaningfully less personality-dependent on a single staff researcher.

Infrastructure & Grid Policy

FERCSix tailored "show cause" orders give AI data centres a federal fast-lane to the grid

Voted 18 June 2026 · Primary: FERC newsroom · TechCrunch · ENR · American Action Forum

On Thursday 18 June, the Federal Energy Regulatory Commission voted 5–0 to issue tailored show-cause orders to each of the country's six big regional grid operators — PJM, MISO, SPP, CAISO, ISO-NE and NYISO — directing them either to defend their existing large-load interconnection frameworks or to propose binding reforms on tight, federally-mandated timelines. Each operator must file a reliability report inside 30 days showing how it will secure generation capacity for AI-data-centre-class loads, and a full interconnection-reform filing inside 60 days. Chair Laura Swett called the action "a national priority," a direct echo of DOE Secretary Chris Wright's May letter requesting accelerated treatment for AI data centres. The five reform categories cover: efficient transmission-service application and study processes; cost-shift prevention with transparency into transmission costs; co-location and behind-the-meter generation; new transmission services for flexible large loads; and a study process for generators electrically proximate to large or co-located loads. The substantive shift is that federal energy regulation is now mechanically aligned with federal AI policy: the same week the White House is negotiating restoration of Fable 5, FERC is removing the most binding non-compute constraint on the AI capex programmes that depend on it. For builders, the downstream effect over 2026–2027 should be visible in shortened interconnection queues at greenfield data-centre sites, especially in PJM and ERCOT-adjacent SPP zones.

Week of 15–21 June 2026 · Primary: huggingface_hub GitHub releases · huggingface.co/blog/huggingface-hub-v1 · State of Open Source Spring 2026

Hugging Face shipped two structural-trust upgrades this week. huggingface_hub v1.0 finalised the platform's primary Python client at a stable major-version boundary — five years after the project's first commit — with a hardened auth model, granular fine-grained token scopes, and a default async surface. More consequential for CI/CD: Hugging Face rolled out secret-less Trusted Publishing from GitHub Actions, GitLab CI and arbitrary custom CI systems, so a workflow can publish to a Hub repo using workflow-identity federation rather than a long-lived API token in repo secrets. Enterprise organisations on Hugging Face also gained service accounts — dedicated, organisation-owned identities for CI/CD and automation that are not tied to individual users and can be managed with fine-grained tokens. Combined with last week's Hugging Face Serge launch, the platform is now visibly hardening its developer-trust surface against the kind of supply-chain risk that has been the loudest open-weights critique post-Mythos.
