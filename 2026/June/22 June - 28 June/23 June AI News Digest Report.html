Sakana AIFugu technical report: an orchestrator that routes across a swappable pool of frontier LLMs, tops several benchmarks without Fable 5

arXiv 19 June 2026 · Sakana AI blog, VentureBeat, MarkTechPost

Yujin Tang and 13 co-authors at Sakana AI released the Fugu technical report on 19 June. Fugu is a family of orchestrator language models that decide when to answer directly versus assemble and coordinate a team of frontier LLMs behind a single OpenAI-compatible endpoint. Training combines large-scale fine-tuning, evolutionary algorithms and RL on top of two prior ICLR 2026 papers (TRINITY and Conductor). Fugu-Ultra tops four coding benchmarks, CharXiv Reasoning and Humanity's Last Exam; the base Fugu leads SciCode, τ³ Banking and Long Context Reasoning — reportedly matching Anthropic Fable 5 / Mythos Preview without including either in the routing pool (notable given the ongoing Fable 5 export-control fallout).

Google DeepMind"How Transparent is DiffusionGemma?" — mech-interp finds diffusion LLMs roughly as monitorable as autoregressive Gemma 4

arXiv 18 June 2026, trending 19–22 June · LessWrong / Alignment Forum

Joshua Engels, Callum McDougall, Bilal Chughtai, Janos Kramar, Senthoran Rajamanoharan, Arthur Conmy, Rohin Shah, Neel Nanda and collaborators at Google DeepMind (joint interpretability + text-diffusion teams) ask whether text-diffusion models are inherently less monitorable than autoregressive ones. They decompose transparency into variable transparency (interpreting intermediate snapshots) and algorithmic transparency (reconstructing the process). Headline finding: DiffusionGemma is similarly monitorable to Gemma 4, and treating intermediate states as interpretable reduces opaque serial depth to just 1.1× that of Gemma 4. Case studies surface non-chronological reasoning, token/sequence smearing and intermediate-context reasoning — useful priors for auditing the diffusion-style models now landing in transformers v5.11.0.

MicrosoftTypeScript 7.0 RC ships the Go-native compiler — ~10× faster, VS Code self-build 77s → 7s

18 June 2026 (RC), uptake through 19–23 June · Microsoft TypeScript DevBlog

Microsoft published the TypeScript 7.0 Release Candidate on 18 June, available immediately as npm install -D typescript@rc. The headline change: the compiler itself is now written in Go rather than TypeScript/JavaScript, reportedly running ~10× faster than 6.0 thanks to native code plus shared-memory parallelism. PM Daniel Rosenwasser said the port preserves identical type-checking semantics — it's a port, not a rewrite. The Go compiler cuts a VS Code self-build from ~77 s to ~7 s; Microsoft expects GA within roughly one month. A stable programmatic API won't land until 7.1.

Node.jsCoordinated 22.23.0 / 24.17.0 / 26.3.1 security release patches 12 CVEs, 2 HIGH

Advisory 18 June 2026; remediation through 19–23 June · Node.js blog

The Node.js Release Working Group shipped coordinated security releases across the 22.x (Jod), 24.x (Krypton) and 26.x (Current) lines covering 12 CVEs. Two are HIGH: CVE-2026-48933, a WebCrypto integer overflow that crashes the process when subtle.encrypt() receives input a multiple of 2 GiB (fixed by Filip Skokan); and CVE-2026-48618, a TLS wildcard-depth authentication bypass caused by a Unicode dot-separator hostname-normalization mismatch (fixed by Matteo Collina). The remaining 10 cover HTTP/2 memory growth, proxy credential leaks via ERR_PROXY_TUNNEL, an mTLS SNI case-sensitivity bypass, and several --permission model escapes. Dependency bumps: llhttp 9.4.2, nghttp2 1.69.0, OpenSSL 3.5.7.

Security ResearchTenet's "Agentjacking" exploit: 85% success against Claude Code, Cursor and Codex via the Sentry MCP

Public reporting mid–late June 2026 · The New Stack

Tenet Security's "Agentjacking" research demonstrated that a single public Sentry DSN is sufficient to inject malicious "remediation guidance" into error events that AI coding agents — Claude Code, Cursor and Codex via the Sentry MCP — will execute without further user prompting. Tenet reported an 85% success rate across more than 100 tested targets, with 2,388 organizations exposing injectable DSNs. Sentry acknowledged the issue but declined a root-cause fix, calling it "technically not defensible," and shipped only a single payload filter.

Policy & Standards

ColoradoSB 26-189 effectively guts the Colorado AI Act before its 30 June effective date arrives

Deadline week of 30 June 2026; legislative action 14 May 2026 · Hunton Privacy Blog, Colorado General Assembly

Colorado's landmark SB 24-205 AI Act — the only comprehensive cross-sector state AI law in the U.S. — was originally set to take effect 30 June 2026. On 14 May 2026, Governor Jared Polis signed SB 26-189, which pushed the effective date to 1 January 2027 and stripped the risk-based framework: the duty of care to prevent algorithmic discrimination, deployer obligations to maintain risk-management programs, mandatory impact assessments and certain AG reporting are all gone, replaced by a narrower disclosure regime for automated decision-making. A federal magistrate judge separately stayed enforcement of Colorado's anti-discrimination AI rules on 27 April 2026. The deadline week passing without enforcement removes the most aggressive state-level AI civil-rights regime in the country.
