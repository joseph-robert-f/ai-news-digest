Anthropic tells Senate that Alibaba/Qwen ran the largest known distillation attack on Claude

In a letter dated 10 June and reported publicly on 24–25 June, Anthropic told Senate Banking Committee chair Tim Scott and ranking member Elizabeth Warren that operators affiliated with Alibaba's Qwen AI lab generated 28.8 million fraudulent Claude exchanges across roughly 25,000 sockpuppet accounts to extract software-engineering and agentic-reasoning capabilities.

  • Campaign window: 22 April → 5 June 2026; Anthropic frames it as adversarial distillation — training a weaker model on the outputs of a frontier model — and the largest such operation it has documented.
  • Companion ask: tighter export controls on AI model access and mandatory high-volume API anomaly screening; senators Bill Hagerty (R-TN) and Andy Kim (D-NJ) are drafting an amendment to defense legislation that would let Treasury sanction implicated entities.
  • Context: Anthropic disclosed three smaller "industrial-scale" distillation campaigns from DeepSeek, Moonshot and MiniMax in February; the Alibaba operation is the first directly named to Congress.
  • The letter lands two weeks before Anthropic's targeted IPO roadshow and arrives the same week Claude saw its 10th outage in three weeks (reported in the 24 June digest).

OpenAI & Broadcom unveil "Jalapeño," OpenAI's first custom-designed inference accelerator

OpenAI and Broadcom jointly announced Jalapeño, an LLM-optimized "Intelligence Processor" — OpenAI's first custom silicon — designed around its own inference workloads. Broadcom CEO Hock Tan hand-delivered engineering samples to Sam Altman and Greg Brockman at OpenAI HQ.

  • Reticle-sized ASIC; co-developed from initial design to tape-out in ~nine months, which Tom's Hardware called "possibly the fastest high-performance ASIC cycle ever."
  • OpenAI says it used its own models to accelerate floorplanning, verification and RTL work — the first frontier-scale chip publicly credited to AI-assisted design.
  • Roadmap: small prototype cluster late-2026, production ramp in 2027, full-scale 10 GW deployment with Microsoft & partners across 2028–2029.
  • Strategic signal: pairs with the SpaceX-Reflection AI compute deal from earlier this week (covered 24 Jun) — frontier labs continue locking down captive, specialized inference capacity.

Node.js ships June security releases across 22.x / 24.x / 26.x

EmberZNet Zigbee stack: cluster of new CVEs disclosed 25 June

Silicon Labs' EmberZNet Zigbee stack (v9.0.2 and earlier) had a cluster of memory-safety CVEs published on 25 June 2026, several reachable from malformed over-the-air messages. The bugs land in shipped firmware across a long tail of Zigbee-based smart-home and industrial endpoints.

  • Highlights: CVE-2026-47151 — out-of-bounds write in Door Lock schedule state; -47150 — OOB write to state table; -47149 — OOB reads triggering process termination; -47148 — read past message payload; -47145 — malformed Color Control messages causing process termination.
  • Hub vendors using EmberZNet need to coordinate fleet OTA rollouts; many devices ship without robust update channels, so the practical patch window is long.
  • Backdrop: June 2026 Patch Tuesday already set a 2026 record at 200 CVEs — 33 critical, 28 of those critical RCEs — including CVE-2026-48579, an unauthenticated info-disclosure in Exchange Online (CVSS 9.1).
