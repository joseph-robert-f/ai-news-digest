Top Story — Anthropic Goes Public on "AI for Science"

Anthropic"The Briefing: AI for Science" — Jumper's first public appearance, VirBench + BioMysteryBench released, Bristol Myers Squibb live

30 June 2026 · Primary sources: anthropic.com event page; anthropic.com/news (accelerating-scientific-research, claude-for-life-sciences)

Anthropic on Tuesday 30 June ran The Briefing: AI for Science, a livestreamed event at 10:00 AM PT bringing together company leadership, pharma/biotech executives, and research-institution partners. The headline is the first public appearance of John Jumper — the AlphaFold co-creator and 2024 Nobel laureate — since his 19 June move from Google DeepMind. The event ships two new public artefacts. VirBench is a 120-query viral-sequence retrieval benchmark spanning 40 pathogens; six frontier models tested without specialised tooling scored from 16.9% (Claude Sonnet 4) to 91.3% (GPT‑5.5). The co-developed NCBI gget virus deterministic tool, built jointly with the National Center for Biotechnology Information, lifts every model above 92% — Sonnet 4 jumps from 16.9% to 92.8%. BioMysteryBench is a 99-question expert-curated bioinformatics set; the human reference solved 76/99, and Claude Mythos Preview averaged 82.6%. The customer-side anchor is Bristol Myers Squibb, deploying Claude across R&D and manufacturing, with additional named partners across the Allen Institute and Howard Hughes Medical Institute strands launched 2 February 2026. Strategically, the event lands the same week as the 27 June partial restoration of Mythos 5 to US critical infrastructure: Anthropic is staking the public framing of its strongest model on science deployments through trusted institutions, not consumer reach — a different posture than the GPT‑5.6 Sol/Terra/Luna tiering OpenAI previewed 26 June.

AI Infrastructure & Compute Markets

Google × MetaGoogle caps Meta's Gemini API access over compute shortages — Meta accelerates in-house Muse Spark

28 June 2026 (FT scoop) · 29 June broad re-coverage · Primary sources: Financial Times via CNBC; Forbes; Data Center Dynamics

The Financial Times reported on Sunday 28 June, with CNBC and Forbes carrying the story into Monday, that Google has put hard limits on Meta's use of the Gemini API after Meta requested more capacity than Alphabet could supply. Per the reporting, the constraint was first imposed around March 2026 and has disrupted and delayed some of Meta's internal AI workflows. Meta has reportedly instructed employees to be more token-efficient and is actively shifting workloads to its in-house Muse Spark model — the foundation model launched out of Meta Superintelligence Labs — reducing reliance on Gemini for internal tooling. The cross-current with Friday's SpaceX–Reflection compute deal: Google itself agreed earlier in June to pay SpaceX ~$920 M per month for ~110,000 Nvidia GPUs housed inside xAI's Colossus, language Google has openly described as a "bridge" for Gemini Enterprise demand. The macro signal is clean: the supply-side bottleneck is real enough that the second-largest hyperscaler can no longer fully sell its frontier-model API to the third-largest, and the world's largest social platform's response is to build its own.

South Korea$576 B / 800 trillion-won national chip plan — Samsung + SK Hynix each build two new Gwangju fabs

29 June 2026 · Primary sources: CNBC; CNN Business; Business Standard; Rappler

President Lee Jae Myung on Monday 29 June unveiled a sweeping industrial strategy that combines semiconductor, AI, and infrastructure spend into a single national programme totalling $576 B (~800 trillion won). The flagship piece: Samsung Electronics and SK Hynix will each build two new semiconductor fabrication plants in the southwestern city of Gwangju, with the Samsung Chairman Lee Jae-yong separately confirming the company's siting. Funding is targeted at high-bandwidth memory (HBM), next-generation memory, edge AI silicon, advanced defence semiconductors, and robotics. The structural read is the part to flag for AI investors: SK Hynix is Nvidia's lead HBM supplier — the bottleneck constraint that has rationed the entire frontier-training stack through 2025–26 — and Samsung is the closest credible second source. A national policy programme of this scale targeted directly at HBM supply is the most concrete sovereign answer yet to the compute scarcity story driving the rest of today's digest (the Google–Meta cap; the SpaceX–Reflection $6.3 B contract; the Oracle 10‑K $55.7 B FY26 capex line; and the still-suspended Fable 5).

Valuation context: Tenstorrent raised at $3.2 B on its $800 M round in 2025 — a deal at $10 B would represent a ~3× markup in roughly 12 months. Intel is reported to have also expressed interest, creating bidding-dynamic risk. Neither party has commented on the record; both Reuters and the FT are tracking. For supply-side AI investors, this is the largest pending non-Nvidia AI-silicon M&A.

Security, Policy & Regulatory

Anthropic / Commerce Dept.Mythos 5 partially restored to 100+ trusted US critical-infrastructure orgs — Fable 5 still suspended worldwide

27 June 2026 announcement; 28–29 June industry coverage · Primary sources: Anthropic newsroom (Claude Fable 5 and Mythos 5); Fortune; GovConWire; TechTimes

Anthropic confirmed on Saturday 27 June that the US Commerce Department has notified it that Mythos 5 — its strongest cybersecurity model, suspended worldwide on 12 June under the unprecedented frontier-AI export-control order — can be redeployed to a defined set of US organisations that operate and defend critical infrastructure. Anthropic is publicly framing the scope as "100+ trusted organisations", the first crack in the directive since 12 June. The qualifications matter: Mythos 5 is not generally available, access is subject to Washington's new frontier-AI review process, and Fable 5 — the developer-facing model that millions had been using before the order — remains suspended worldwide, with the original civil and criminal penalties still in force. The companion piece, per TechTimes' 28 June reporting, is that OpenAI's GPT‑5.6 (Sol/Terra/Luna tiering previewed 26 June) is being walked through the same customer-by-customer government-gated process: limited preview, no general release until DC approves. The structural read for builders: the export-control posture from 12 June is becoming the steady-state policy regime — not a one-off — and is now applied to both labs symmetrically. The Anthropic-vs-DOD lawsuit, separately, continues on a distinct legal track and has not been settled by the Commerce thaw.
