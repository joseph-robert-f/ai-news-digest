AnthropicAnthropic files a confidential draft S-1 with the SEC at a $965 B mark and ~$47 B annualized revenue

On Monday 1 June, Anthropic, PBC confirmed it had submitted a confidential draft registration statement on Form S-1 to the U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission for a proposed initial public offering of its common stock — the formal step that puts Anthropic ahead of OpenAI on the public-markets timeline by roughly 10 days. The filing comes six days after the company quietly closed the $65 B Series H first reported by Bloomberg on 12 May (and projected to close the week of 26 May in our 27 May digest); the financing settled at a higher final cut than the originally-flagged $30–$50 B band, pushing the post-money mark to $965 B — the highest valuation ever assigned to a private company at the moment a draft S-1 was filed. In its own newsroom note Anthropic disclosed an annualized revenue run-rate of ~$47 B, up from the ~$30 B figure circulating in 25 May's coverage and from $9 B at end-2025. Under SEC Rule 15a-6 the draft will sit non-public until at least 15 days before any roadshow; the practical implication is that the first audited Anthropic P&L the market sees is now a Q3-2026 event. Strategically, by going first Anthropic also sets the comparable that OpenAI's 22 May confidential filing (covered in 25 May digest) will be re-priced against once both prospectuses go public.

Policy & Regulatory Signals

White HouseTrump signs Promoting Advanced AI Innovation and Security EO — voluntary 30-day pre-release review, AI cybersecurity clearinghouse

2 June 2026 · Primary source: WhiteHouse.gov presidential actions / NBC News / NPR

President Trump signed an executive order on Tuesday 2 June titled "Promoting Advanced Artificial Intelligence Innovation and Security" that marks the administration's first explicit move toward a federal AI-safety regime since the January 2025 rescission of the Biden AI EO. The order has three operative provisions. (1) It invites — not requires — AI developers to voluntarily submit their most capable forthcoming models for federal evaluation up to 30 days before public release; an earlier draft circulating last week had specified 90 days, a number the final text materially shortened under industry pressure. (2) It directs federal agencies, led by the NSA and the NIST AI Safety Institute, to publish standardized cyber-capability benchmarks for model evaluation. (3) It establishes an "AI cybersecurity clearinghouse" for vulnerability disclosures discovered by AI systems — an explicit nod to Anthropic's Project Glasswing findings (10,000+ critical vulnerabilities, covered in our 27 May digest) and to the Palo Alto Networks Mythos advisory cycle. Critically, the order expressly prohibits any reading that would create a "mandatory governmental licensing, preclearance, or permitting requirement" — the cleanest signal yet that the U.S. is choosing a voluntary-cooperation track in deliberate divergence from the EU AI Act timeline locked in by the 7 May Omnibus (see 28 May digest). Reactions inside the labs split along the same lines as the 28 May Altman/Amodei jobs walk-back: OpenAI and Microsoft welcomed the voluntary framing in same-day statements; Anthropic publicly aligned with the cybersecurity clearinghouse but reiterated that Claude Mythos will not be released to the public regardless of the federal evaluation track.

Frontier Models & Build 2026

MicrosoftMAI-Thinking-1 and MAI-Code-1-Flash: Microsoft's first reasoning and coding models trained without OpenAI distillation

2 June 2026 · Primary source: Microsoft Build 2026 keynote / Microsoft.ai blog / CNBC

At Build 2026's opening keynote at Fort Mason in San Francisco on Tuesday 2 June, Microsoft AI chief Mustafa Suleyman unveiled seven new in-house models — the largest single-day model release in the company's history — led by MAI-Thinking-1, its first reasoning model and the first frontier-class Microsoft model trained without distillation from any third-party model, explicitly including OpenAI's GPT family. The technical disclosure is unusually concrete: 35 B active parameters within roughly 1 T total parameters in a sparse mixture-of-experts architecture, a 256 K-token context window, and training exclusively on commercially-licensed enterprise data. On benchmarks Microsoft published in the technical brief, MAI-Thinking-1 hits 97.0% on AIME 2025, 94.5% on AIME 2026, and on SWE-Bench Pro Microsoft says it matches Claude Opus 4.6 on coding tasks while running roughly 4× cheaper per inference. The companion MAI-Code-1-Flash is a 5 B-parameter coding model that began rolling out to every GitHub Copilot tier — Free, Pro, Pro+, and Max — today; Microsoft says it beats Claude Haiku 4.5 on SWE-Bench Pro by 16 percentage points while using 60% fewer tokens on complex tasks. The strategic message read by analysts on the floor: Microsoft is no longer comfortable underwriting OpenAI's pricing power for high-volume code completion, and the unit economics of Copilot at ~$50 B run-rate demand a model stack it controls end-to-end. Rayfin, a separately-announced OSS SDK/CLI that generates a typed governed backend (DB, auth, storage, policies) deployed to Microsoft Fabric, ships in preview the same day.

AnthropicClaude Sonnet 4 and Opus 4 retire from the API on 15 June 2026; Opus 4.8 dynamic-workflow renamed to ultracode

2–3 June 2026 · Primary source: Claude API release notes (platform.claude.com) / Anthropic Help Center / GitHub Changelog

Anthropic posted a hard 15 June 2026 retirement date for Claude Sonnet 4 and Claude Opus 4 on the Claude API — the original GA models from the May–August 2024 wave. Customers are routed to Claude Sonnet 4.6 and Claude Opus 4.8 respectively; Opus 4.8 (GA on GitHub Copilot since 28 May) now defaults to high-effort thinking and gains dynamic workflows that let Claude scaffold and orchestrate tens to hundreds of sub-agents in the background against a single user goal. In a small but operationally meaningful change for Claude Code users, the trigger keyword for that behavior has been renamed from the heavily-overloaded word workflow to ultracode — the literal token "workflow" in user prompts no longer activates a dynamic run, eliminating an entire class of accidental long-running sessions. The Anthropic Developer Platform also added a new max_tokens parameter on the advisor tool to cap output, reduce latency, and lower token costs, and Claude Code shipped an Auto mode on Bedrock, Vertex, and Microsoft Foundry for Opus 4.7 and 4.8 — the Foundry integration is fresh from Build 2026 and is the first cross-platform Anthropic shipping vehicle that mirrors GitHub Copilot's distribution. Note: the model-retirement window is the cleanest pre-IPO inventory-tidy yet observed in the field, removing two SKUs from the support matrix exactly two weeks before the S-1 likely becomes public.

Developer Platforms & Agent Frameworks

MicrosoftMicrosoft Agent Framework 1.0 hits GA with native Agent-to-Agent (A2A) protocol support across .NET and Python

2 June 2026 · Primary source: Microsoft Foundry blog / devblogs.microsoft.com

The agentic-development surface at Build 2026 is anchored on the Microsoft Agent Framework 1.0 reaching general availability, replacing the RC line that had been in preview since February 2026. The 1.0 release ships production-grade agent orchestration for both .NET and Python and is the first major orchestration framework to ship native support for the Agent-to-Agent (A2A) protocol at GA, allowing agents built on entirely different runtimes (Semantic Kernel, AutoGen, custom .NET, LangGraph-compatible Python, Claude harnesses via MCP-A2A bridges) to discover, authenticate, and coordinate with each other. In the same Foundry update, Microsoft moved three previously-preview capabilities from Copilot Studio into GA: computer-using agents (CUAs), agent-to-agent communication, and real-time voice; Foundry agents can now publish directly into Microsoft Teams and Microsoft 365 Copilot. The combination matters because it is the first end-to-end vendor stack where the orchestration framework, the model (MAI-Thinking-1), the runtime (Foundry), the distribution surface (Teams/M365), and the gateway (Foundry's model router) are owned by the same vendor — effectively closing the loop that OpenRouter's $113 M Series B identified as the new infrastructure category. For independent agent developers, the practical bar to be considered "enterprise-grade" on Azure now includes A2A interop on day one.

The implication for AI-research funding: scientific-throughput benchmarks — not just MMLU or AIME — are now the metrics DOE evaluations are using to justify next-cycle exascale appropriations.
