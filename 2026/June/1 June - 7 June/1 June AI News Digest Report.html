NVIDIAOpen Agent Development Platform: 16 enterprise-software leaders ship on NemoClaw + Nemotron + OpenShell

1 June 2026 · Primary source: NVIDIA Newsroom / NVIDIA Investor Relations / NVIDIA Blog

Opening the GTC Taipei 2026 keynote at the Taipei Music Center on Monday morning, NVIDIA announced the Open Agent Development Platform — positioned by Jensen Huang as the substrate for "the next industrial revolution in knowledge work." The platform is anchored on three new components: the NVIDIA NemoClaw blueprint family (specialised enterprise-agent blueprints), the Nemotron 3 Ultra open-weights model (300+ output tokens/sec, ~30% lower cost than leading alternatives), and a new OpenShell secure runtime for long-running agentic execution. Launch partners — rare in their breadth — include Adobe, Atlassian, Amdocs, Box, Cadence, Cisco, Cohesity, CrowdStrike, Dassault Systèmes, IQVIA, Red Hat, SAP, Salesforce, Siemens, ServiceNow, and Synopsys. Cadence, Dassault Systèmes, Siemens and Synopsys are the first to use NemoClaw to build "autonomous AI engineers" for simulation and verification workflows, with NVIDIA claiming order-of-magnitude compression of multi-week tasks. CrowdStrike and Palantir are running long-context Nemotron-powered agents in cyber and operational decisioning. The strategic shift in the announcement is structural: NVIDIA is pricing and licensing CUDA-X libraries as agent-callable tools rather than developer SDKs — turning the entire NVIDIA stack into a tool catalog any agent harness can invoke through a single contract.

Policy & Regulation

White HouseTrump scraps signing of landmark AI executive order — CISA / OSTP review pipeline left in limbo

21–22 May 2026 · Primary source: NBC News / NPR / Axios / Washington Post

In a late-afternoon reversal on Thursday 21 May, President Donald Trump abruptly cancelled the planned signing of a landmark AI executive order — an order Axios subsequently published the text of, showing two main sections: (1) a frontier-model testing-and-vetting program designed to be run on a voluntary basis with leading AI labs through CISA, the National Cyber Director's office, and the Office of Science and Technology Policy (OSTP); and (2) a cybersecurity track that would have hardened federal AI deployments against adversarial use. Trump told reporters at the signing ceremony that he had pulled it because it "could interfere with American competitiveness": "We're leading China, we're leading everybody, and I don't want to do anything that's going to get in the way of that lead." Reporting from The Hill and the Washington Post describes a last-minute lobbying push by White House AI & crypto advisor David Sacks and incoming industry voices against the testing-regime language. The practical outcome: the only federal AI-risk framework still in force is the March 2026 National AI Policy Framework, with no enforcement teeth. US-China AI rules talks led by Treasury Secretary Bessent (14 May) are now the de-facto policy channel, while the EU AI Act Omnibus (covered in the 28 May digest) accelerates around them.

Hugging FaceOpen-source LLMs now plug directly into GitHub Copilot Chat in VS Code

27 May 2026 · Primary source: Hugging Face / DevOps.com

Hugging Face announced on 27 May that its catalogue of open-source LLMs — including DeepSeek-V4-Pro, Qwen 3.7-Max, the Llama family, and the broader long tail of community models — can now be selected directly from the GitHub Copilot Chat model picker in VS Code via a Hugging Face extension. The extension routes through hundreds of inference providers, meaning developers can switch models without leaving the editor and without provisioning separate API keys for each one. The practical implication for enterprise teams: the choice of "which model powers Copilot Chat" no longer requires an admin policy to be edited in advance for every model swap — it can be done per-task by a developer, with consumption flowing through the standard Hugging Face Inference Providers contract. Combined with NVIDIA's parallel push to make Nemotron 3 Ultra freely available on Hugging Face, the open-weights stack now has a credible distribution path into the single most-used editor on Earth.
