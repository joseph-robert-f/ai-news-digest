Research

Google ResearchTurboQuant at ICLR 2026: 6× KV-cache compression with near-zero quality loss, no training required

ICLR 2026 / posted to arXiv 2025; community implementations landed in last 48h · Primary source: Google Research blog

Google Research's TurboQuant — presented at ICLR 2026 by Zandieh, Daliri, Hadian and Mirrokni — gives LLM inference a near-information-theoretic-optimal vector-quantization scheme for the KV cache, the single largest memory bottleneck in long-context inference. The technique combines PolarQuant (a rotation-based coordinate transform) with a 1-bit QJL residual correction to compress KV vectors to 3 bits/coordinate with effectively zero accuracy loss, delivering at least 6× memory reduction and up to 8× faster attention on NVIDIA H100s. Critically, the method is data-oblivious and requires no training and no calibration data — it processes each vector online as it's written to cache. Within 48 hours of broader ICLR press coverage this week, multiple open-source community implementations appeared on GitHub (including a llama.cpp port targeting Mistral-7B that reports 3.8–5.7× memory reduction with no quality loss). The practical impact: production 1M-context workloads (e.g. Claude Opus 4.8's default 1 M context, or Gemma 4 12B running locally) become substantially cheaper at the same quality, which has direct implications for the Maia/Trainium/Blackwell inference economics conversation.
